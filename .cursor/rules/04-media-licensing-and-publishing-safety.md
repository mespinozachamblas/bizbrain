# Media Licensing And Publishing Safety

- Treat Google Images, Pinterest, and similar search/discovery surfaces as reference-only unless the original asset page and license are independently verified.
- Prefer own images, explicitly licensed stock or open-license assets, and first-party generated assets for publishable media candidates. Typical preferred libraries include Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay, Openverse, and Wikimedia Commons, subject to per-asset review where required.
- Do not present a third-party image as publishable without provenance metadata: source type, origin URL, license label when known, attribution requirement when known, and review status.
- Do not assume discoverability equals reusability.
- Flag logos, trademarks, celebrity or public-figure likenesses, screenshots, editorial photos, and identifiable people for explicit human review before publication.
- Generated visuals may use references for inspiration, but must not rely on unverified third-party assets as if they were licensed source files.
- When in doubt, label the candidate `reference-only` or `review-required`, not `publishable`.
